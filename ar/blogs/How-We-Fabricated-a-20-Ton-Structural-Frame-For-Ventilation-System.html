How We Fabricated a 20-Ton Structural Frame: A Case Study

Table of Contents

  • Client Background
  • Project Scope & Challenges
  • Design & Engineering
  • Fabrication & QA Process
  • Timeline & Delivery
  • Results & Client Feedback
  • Lessons Learned

Client Background

The client is a leading player in the industrial ventilation systems sector, known for their advanced engineering and high standards. After thoroughly evaluating our metal fabrication capabilities—including equipment performance, process integrity, quality control, and logistics—they selected us as their fabrication partner for key components in a paper mill ventilation system.

Once the contract was initiated, our team began coordinated production, including on-site welding, painting, component sorting, and professional packing—executed to international logistics and QA standards.

Project Scope & Challenges

This project involved high surface finish requirements, a large quantity of parts, a tight delivery window, and extensive painting and inspection demands. To meet the client’s overseas usage requirements, multiple departments—production, sales, QA, and engineering—collaborated closely to implement a batch-based production workflow covering cutting, drilling, welding, sandblasting, and painting.

Thanks to careful planning and execution, we were able to boost production efficiency without compromising the high standards expected from a structural steel fabrication company. The client’s onsite engineers praised our welding quality and packaging precision.

Design & Engineering

Our engineering team translated the client’s CAD models into optimized fabrication-ready files. Tolerances were reviewed carefully to ensure precision. Key improvements were made to weld joint design and part fit-up to streamline downstream processes. We applied 3D modeling in fabrication to simulate fit and assembly ahead of production.

Fabrication & QA Process

We adopted a phased production approach:

  • Laser Cutting & Drilling: For accuracy and repeatability
  • Manual & Robotic Welding: For structural integrity and repeatable weld quality
  • Surface Prep & Paint: Blasted to spec and coated in two layers for corrosion resistance
  • Quality Control: Dimensional inspections, weld visual inspection, and dry film thickness checks per client standards

As a QA-driven metal fabricator, we followed strict process documentation and kept all inspection records traceable for export compliance.

Timeline & Delivery

The full scope was completed in under 5 weeks—on time, within budget, and fully export-compliant. Parts were staged and packed per shipping specs, then delivered to the client’s logistics partner with full QA documentation.

Results & Client Feedback

The project was a success on all fronts. The structural frames met all performance and finish criteria. Our heavy metal fabrication service exceeded expectations on both quality and delivery. The client noted our efficient workflow and strong team coordination, calling us "a dependable partner for international fabrication needs."

Lessons Learned

Key takeaways from this project:

  • Front-loaded planning reduces mid-project changes
  • Clear internal communication supports fast-track timelines
  • Robust QA systems ensure compliance and client trust

At every step, we applied our core strengths in industrial structural fabrication to deliver value, performance, and peace of mind. That’s what defines our approach to every project we take on.
