Common Certifications in Metal Fabrication

When you're sourcing a certified metal fabrication company, these are the standards to look for:

  • ISO 9001: Global standard for quality management systems
  • AWS (American Welding Society): Defines the benchmark for weld quality and procedure
  • ASME (American Society of Mechanical Engineers): Standards for pressure vessels and mechanical components
  • NADCAP: Accreditation for aerospace and defense-grade processes
  • CE Marking: Required for parts sold in the European Economic Area

What These Certifications Require

Achieving and maintaining these certifications isn't just paperwork—it’s a demonstration of real process discipline. Here’s what’s typically involved:

  • Documented procedures for fabrication, inspection, and traceability
  • Trained and qualified staff for welding, machining, and testing
  • Regular third-party audits and internal reviews
  • Controlled materials, calibrated equipment, and clean documentation trails

Our approach to quality-certified metal fabrication goes beyond compliance—we build it into every weld, cut, and part we ship.

Why Buyers Should Care

If you’re buying fabricated metal parts, certifications are more than just letters—they’re risk reducers. Working with a compliance-driven fabrication partner means:

  • You meet your own regulatory and spec requirements faster
  • You reduce the risk of failure, rework, or recalls
  • You get documented proof of quality and traceability

Especially in industries like aerospace, energy, and medical devices, certified practices are essential for both legal and operational success.
